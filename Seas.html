Seas

What & How

Oceans, vital for biodiversity, face increasing threats from human activities. Overfishing and pollution, including plastic waste and oil spills, disrupt marine ecosystems. Climate change compounds issues with rising sea levels, impacting coastal habitats crucial for various species.

Notable examples include the decline of sea turtle populations due to habitat loss and the vulnerability of coral reefs, like the Great Barrier Reef, to bleaching events. Rising sea levels exacerbate challenges, affecting both marine and terrestrial ecosystems in low-lying coastal areas.

Comprehensive conservation efforts are imperative. Sustainable fishing, marine protected areas, and plastic pollution reduction initiatives are crucial to mitigate threats. Learning from successes, like humpback whale population recovery after hunting restrictions, provides hope for the resilience of marine ecosystems amid climate-related challenges.
