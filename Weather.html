Weather

What & How

Weather, a dynamic force influenced by human activities, presents challenges for ecosystems. Climate change brings extreme events like prolonged droughts and intensified storms, disrupting natural balances. Rising temperatures compound issues, causing heat stress on coral reefs and altering migration and breeding patterns.

Examples include shifts in bird migration and changes in flowering seasons, illustrating the interconnectedness of ecosystems. Proactive conservation efforts are crucial. Initiatives for climate resilience, habitat restoration, and sustainable land management help adapt to evolving weather conditions. Learning from successful interventions, such as wetland restoration to buffer against storms, offers valuable insights for navigating the complexities of our changing climate.
